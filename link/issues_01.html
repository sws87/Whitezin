Issue V: Shame   


The ortolan has been described to be the most decadent, rare and precious meal one could experience in a lifetime. The mythic ortolan gained fame when Fracnois Mitterand had them specially prepared for his last meal. The traditional way to eat the bird is to cover your head with a napkin to “shield your shame from God.” This custom fascinated me, how this delicacy required hidden consumption due to the diner’s ignominy. However, in another region of the world, the ortolan, otherwise known as bul-bul in the Middle East, is not consumed in the same way. There is no shame in the act.
